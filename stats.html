How is this calculated?
About the PSAI
The Puget Sound Academic Index (PSAI) is an objective, un-biased 1-to-10 rating designed to show overall public school district proficiency across King and Snohomish Counties.

Unlike traditional real estate portals that rely on commercial third-party algorithms, the PSAI is calculated using direct, un-modified public records from the Washington Office of Superintendent of Public Instruction (OSPI).

How It's Calculated: We take the literal percentage of district students who met or exceeded grade-level standards on the state's latest Smarter Balanced Assessments in Mathematics and English Language Arts (Reading), average them together, and normalize them onto a standard 10-point scale.

What do these numbers mean?
What "Per 1,000 Residents" Means: This normalizes the data so a buyer can fairly compare a small town to a massive metropolis[cite: 45]. For example, a property rate of 20.0 means that for every 1,000 neighbors living in this city, roughly 20 property-related incidents (theft, burglary, vandalism) are recorded annually [cite: 46].

The Crime Categories:
• Violent Crime: Reflects crimes against persons (assault, robbery, tracking infractions) [cite: 47].
• Property Crime: Reflects crimes against structures or goods (burglary, larceny, motor vehicle theft, vandalism)[cite: 48].
